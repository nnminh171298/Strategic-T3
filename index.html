Rules

(Choose ComX or ComO to play with the computer)

X goes first and can play anywhere on the global board.

The relative location of the last move on the local board (3x3 board) is the relative location on the global (9x9 board) of the next move.

If a move is played so that it is to win a local board by the rules of normal tic-tac-toe then it wins that local board.

Once the outcome of a local board is decided (win or draw) no more moves may be played in that board.

If a player is sent to such a board, then that player may play in any other board.

Game play ends when either a player wins the global board or there are no legal moves remaining.

By then, whoever wins more local boards wins the game.
